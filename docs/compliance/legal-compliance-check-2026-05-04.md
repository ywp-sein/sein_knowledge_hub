# Legal Compliance Check Record

Date: 2026-05-04  
Scope: SEiN Knowledge Hub wiki template and legal pages; related SEiN website imprint wording

This record documents a practical compliance review of the static wiki-style website template and legal pages. It is not legal advice. It records what was checked, which sources were used, and what content changes were made.

## Checked Areas

- Imprint/provider identification for a Germany-based public website.
- Privacy transparency for a static GitHub Pages website.
- Use of local storage for the theme setting.
- External links, third-party materials, and source references.
- Reuse terms for original Knowledge Hub content and website code.
- Outdated legal references in the related `sein_web` imprint page.

## Resulting Changes

- The Knowledge Hub privacy pages were expanded with:
  - responsible party details;
  - GitHub Pages access-log notice;
  - legal basis for technically necessary access data;
  - email contact handling;
  - GDPR data-subject rights;
  - Berlin data protection authority contact;
  - official references.
- The Knowledge Hub license pages were expanded with:
  - clearer distinction between original Knowledge Hub text and third-party material;
  - guidance to summarize and link external sources instead of copying them;
  - warning not to reuse logos, photographs, maps, datasets, or downloadable documents unless the source permits it;
  - reliability note that the hub is informational and not legal, medical, social-work, or emergency advice.
- The related `sein_web/legal/imprint.html` page was updated from outdated `TMG` / `RStV` wording to current `DDG` / `MStV` wording.

## Current Template Practice

- Keep a visible `Imprint`, `Privacy Policy`, and `License` page in the sidebar.
- Keep article source references embedded inside the relevant article content.
- Keep third-party source links close to the claims they support.
- Do not present the Knowledge Hub license as applying to third-party content.
- Do not add a cookie banner unless cookies, tracking, analytics, embedded third-party media, or similar consent-relevant services are added.
- If a page includes urgent support information, eligibility rules, opening hours, addresses, phone numbers, or legal-rights information, check current source pages before changing the visible revision timestamp.

## Follow-Up Checklist

- Recheck the privacy page if analytics, forms, donation tools, newsletter tools, embedded media, or external scripts are added.
- Recheck the imprint if the operator, address, email, responsible editor, or publishing purpose changes.
- Recheck license wording if the site starts embedding third-party images, maps, datasets, PDFs, or copied text.
- Keep source references in article pages when facts, organizations, services, laws, policies, phone numbers, or eligibility details are added.
