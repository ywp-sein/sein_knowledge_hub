Homelessness policies in Germany

Selected Germany-level policy developments related to homelessness, housing loss prevention, and data since 2016.

Overview

Homelessness policy in Germany is shared across federal, state, and municipal levels. The federal level has become more visible in recent years through national reporting, housing benefit reform, social housing investment, and the National Action Plan to overcome homelessness by 2030.

Selected timeline

Year Development Why it matters
2016-2020 Growing public and professional pressure for better national data and prevention. Set the background for federal homelessness reporting and more coordinated national policy.
2020 Wohnungslosenberichterstattungsgesetz entered into force. Created a federal statistical basis for measuring homelessness and informing policy.
2022 First federal homelessness report was published. Provided the first Germany-wide overview covering accommodated, hidden, and unsheltered homelessness.
2023 Wohngeld-Plus reform took effect. Expanded and strengthened housing benefit, including heating cost support, as a prevention-oriented housing cost instrument.
2024 Federal cabinet adopted the National Action Plan against Homelessness. Set a national framework for overcoming homelessness by 2030 through cooperation between federal, state, municipal, civil society, research, and housing actors.
2024 onward National Forum against Homelessness and federal competence structures began implementation work. Moves the plan from document to continuing coordination, annual work programs, research, recommendations, and monitoring.
2025-2026 Regular housing benefit adjustment and next federal homelessness report cycle. Shows the shift toward recurring instruments: benefit updates, monitoring, and updated national evidence.

Research notes

This is a selected overview, not a complete legal history. A fuller article should add state-level strategies, municipal accommodation duties, Housing First programs, health access, EU commitments, and civil society critiques of implementation gaps.
