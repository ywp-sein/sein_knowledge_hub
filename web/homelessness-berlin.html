Overview

This issue page gathers public information about homelessness-related support in Berlin and turns it into an easy-to-scan orientation article. The aim is not to replace service providers, but to make the first route through the information easier to understand.

Linked sources should be checked before travel or outreach, because opening times, phone lines, seasonal offers, and eligibility details can change.

Urgent help

In an immediate emergency, use the regular emergency numbers: 112 for medical emergencies or fire, and 110 for police emergencies. For cold-weather risk, Berlin Kältehilfe provides seasonal information about overnight places, night cafes, meeting points, soup kitchens, and cold aid contacts.

Situation First action Notes
Immediate danger Call 112 or 110 Use 112 for medical/fire emergencies and 110 for police emergencies.
Cold weather exposure Check Berlin Kältehilfe Services are seasonal. Ask for consent before calling an outreach bus when possible.
Threatened housing loss Contact Soziale Wohnhilfe District social offices are the official first route for threatened or existing homelessness.

Support routes

A practical route usually begins with safety, then a sleeping place, food and hygiene, health support, documents, and longer-term housing advice. Berlin district offices and specialist counselling services are important because some support depends on legal status, income, district responsibility, or individual circumstances.

People facing homelessness and severe social difficulties may be eligible for personal help under German social law, including support described by Berlin under SGB XII section 67. This page only summarizes the route; applications and case-specific questions should be handled with qualified advice.

Helping responsibly

Helpful action should begin with respect and consent. If direct contact feels safe and appropriate, ask what the person wants before calling services or deciding what they need. Concrete offers such as water, food, a warm drink, looking up a nearby support point, or calling a service can be more useful than general advice.
